# 9. Current

## Given

The charge flowing through the wire is described by the function:

Q(t) = 5t² + 5

We are asked to find the current when:

t = 3 s

---

# Understanding the Problem

Electric current tells us how fast electric charge moves through a wire.

Mathematically, current is the rate of change of charge with respect to time.

This means:

I(t) = dQ/dt

So, to find the current, we must calculate the derivative of the charge function.

---

# Step 1 — Write the Charge Function

Given:

Q(t) = 5t² + 5

This equation tells us how the charge changes over time.

The first term, 5t², changes with time, while the constant 5 does not change.

---

# Step 2 — Differentiate the Function

We now calculate the derivative.

Formula:

I(t) = d/dt (5t² + 5)

Differentiate term by term.

---

## Differentiate the First Term

The derivative of:

5t²

is:

10t

because:

d/dt (t²) = 2t

and:

5 × 2t = 10t

---

## Differentiate the Constant

The derivative of a constant is always zero.

So:

d/dt (5) = 0

---

## Final Expression for Current

Combine the results:

I(t) = 10t

---

# Step 3 — Substitute t = 3 s

Now substitute:

t = 3

I(3) = 10 × 3

I(3) = 30 A

---

# Explanation

The current increases as time increases because the charge function contains t².

This means charge flows faster and faster over time.

At 3 seconds, the current reaches 30 amperes.

---

# Final Answer

The current at t = 3 s is:

30 A
