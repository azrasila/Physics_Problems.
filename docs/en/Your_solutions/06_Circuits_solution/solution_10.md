# 10. Average Current

## Given

A lightning bolt transfers:

Q = 30 Coulombs

to the ground in:

t = 2 milliseconds

We are asked to calculate the average current of the lightning bolt.

---

# Understanding the Problem

Electric current measures how much charge flows per second.

Average current is calculated using:

I = Q / t

where:

- I = current
- Q = charge
- t = time

---

# Step 1 — Convert Milliseconds to Seconds

The formula requires time in seconds.

We know:

1 millisecond = 0.001 seconds

Therefore:

2 milliseconds = 2 × 0.001

t = 0.002 s

---

# Explanation

Unit conversion is very important in physics.

If we forget to convert milliseconds into seconds, the answer will be incorrect.

---

# Step 2 — Use the Current Formula

Formula:

I = Q / t

Substitute the values:

I = 30 / 0.002

---

# Step 3 — Calculate the Result

I = 15000 A

---

# Explanation

The lightning bolt transfers a large amount of charge in a very short time.

Because the time interval is extremely small, the current becomes extremely large.

This is why lightning contains enormous energy and can be very dangerous.

---

# Final Answer

The average current of the lightning bolt is:

15000 A
