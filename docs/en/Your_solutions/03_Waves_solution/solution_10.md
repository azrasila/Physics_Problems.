# Task 10 – Superposition of Wave Sources

## Problem Statement

A wave field is generated by multiple sources:

$$
u(\vec{r},t) = \frac{A}{|\vec{r}-\vec{r_0}|^\alpha} \sin(k |\vec{r} - \vec{r_0}| - \omega t)
$$

Analyze the resulting wave behavior.

---

## Theory

### 1. Radial Wave Propagation

The expression:

$$
|\vec{r} - \vec{r_0}|
$$

represents the distance from the source.

Thus, the wave propagates radially outward.

---

### 2. Amplitude Decay

The factor:

$$
\frac{1}{r^\alpha}
$$

controls how amplitude decreases with distance:

- $\alpha = 0$ → no decay  
- $\alpha = 1$ → physical spherical wave  
- $\alpha = 2$ → rapid decay  

---

### 3. Superposition Principle

For multiple sources:

$$
u_{\text{total}} = \sum_i u_i
$$

Each source contributes independently.

---

## Step-by-Step Analysis

### 1. Define distance

$$
r_i = |\vec{r} - \vec{r_i}|
$$

---

### 2. Write total wave

$$
u(\vec{r},t) = \sum_i \frac{A}{r_i^\alpha} \sin(k r_i - \omega t)
$$

---

### 3. Interference mechanism

At a given point:

- Waves may add constructively  
- Or cancel destructively  

depending on phase differences:

$$
\Delta \phi = k(r_1 - r_2)
$$

---

## Final Result

The wave field is a complex interference pattern formed by:

$$
u = \sum_i \frac{A}{r_i^\alpha} \sin(k r_i - \omega t)
$$

---

## Interpretation

This model explains:

- ripple patterns on water  
- sound interference  
- wave scattering  

The geometry of sources determines the entire pattern.
