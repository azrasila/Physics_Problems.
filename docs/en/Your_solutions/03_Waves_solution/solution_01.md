# Task 01 – Wave Properties

## Problem Statement

A sound wave has frequency $f = 440 \ \text{Hz}$.

Determine its wavelength:
- in air, where $v_{\text{air}} = 343 \ \text{m/s}$,
- in water, where $v_{\text{water}} = 1482 \ \text{m/s}$.

---

## Theory

Waves satisfy a fundamental relationship between speed, frequency, and wavelength:

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

This equation can be interpreted as follows:

- $v$ → how fast the wave propagates,
- $f$ → how many oscillations occur per second,
- $\lambda$ → the spatial length of one cycle.

Rearranging:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

### Key Physical Insight

When a wave enters a different medium:

- The **frequency remains constant** (it is determined by the source),
- The **wave speed changes** (depends on the medium),
- Therefore, the **wavelength must change**.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Wavelength in Air

Substitute known values:

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440}
$$

Compute:

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.780 \ \text{m}
$$

---

### 2. Wavelength in Water

Substitute:

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440}
$$

Compute:

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.368 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78 \ \text{m}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Interpretation

The wavelength in water is much larger than in air. This is because:

- Sound travels **much faster in water**,
- The frequency does **not change**,
- Therefore, the wavelength increases proportionally.

This demonstrates a fundamental principle:

> A wave adjusts its wavelength when entering a new medium while preserving its frequency.
