# Task 11 – Two-Slit Interference

## Problem Statement

Two coherent sources produce waves:

$$
u = u_1 + u_2
$$

Analyze the resulting interference pattern.

---

## Theory

### 1. Individual Waves

Each source emits:

$$
u_1 = \frac{A}{r_1} \sin(k r_1 - \omega t)
$$

$$
u_2 = \frac{A}{r_2} \sin(k r_2 - \omega t)
$$

---

### 2. Path Difference

Define:

$$
\Delta r = r_2 - r_1
$$

This determines phase difference:

$$
\Delta \phi = k \Delta r
$$

---

### 3. Conditions for Interference

#### Constructive interference

$$
\Delta r = n\lambda
$$

#### Destructive interference

$$
\Delta r = \left(n + \frac{1}{2}\right)\lambda
$$

---

## Step-by-Step Analysis

### 1. Total wave

$$
u = u_1 + u_2
$$

---

### 2. Intensity relation

Intensity is proportional to:

$$
I \propto |u|^2
$$

This leads to interference fringes.

---

### 3. Fringe pattern

Maxima occur where:

$$
k(r_2 - r_1) = 2\pi n
$$

---

## Final Result

The interference pattern is determined by:

$$
\Delta r = n\lambda \quad (\text{maxima})
$$

$$
\Delta r = \left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right)\lambda \quad (\text{minima})
$$

---

## Interpretation

This experiment demonstrates the wave nature of light and sound.

Key result:

- Bright fringes → constructive interference  
- Dark fringes → destructive interference  

The spacing of fringes depends on:

- wavelength  
- slit separation  
- distance to screen
