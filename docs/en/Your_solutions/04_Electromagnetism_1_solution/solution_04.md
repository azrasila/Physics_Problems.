# Problem 4 — Electric vs Gravitational Force

## 📘 Theory
Two forces act between an electron and a proton:

Electric force:
Fₑ = k e² / r²  

Gravitational force:
F_g = G mₚ mₑ / r²  

Taking the ratio eliminates distance:

Fₑ / F_g = k e² / (G mₚ mₑ)

---

## 🧮 Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Understand the goal
We compare how strong electric force is relative to gravity.

---

### Step 2: Write the ratio

Fₑ / F_g = (k e²) / (G mₚ mₑ)

---

### Step 3: Substitute constants

k = 9×10⁹  
e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹  
G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹  
mₚ = 1.67×10⁻²⁷  
mₑ = 9.11×10⁻³¹  

---

### Step 4: Compute numerator

k e² ≈ 9×10⁹ × (1.6×10⁻¹⁹)²  
≈ 2.3×10⁻²⁸  

---

### Step 5: Compute denominator

G mₚ mₑ ≈ 6.67×10⁻¹¹ × (1.67×10⁻²⁷)(9.11×10⁻³¹)  
≈ 1.0×10⁻⁶⁷  

---

### Step 6: Divide

Fₑ / F_g ≈ 10³⁹  

---

## ✅ Final Answer
Fₑ / F_g ≈ 10³⁹

---

## 💡 Key Insight
Electric force is **enormously stronger** than gravity at atomic scale.
