# Problem 1 — Coulomb’s Law

## 📘 Theory
Coulomb’s Law describes the force between two point charges:

F = k |q₁q₂| / r²

- The force acts along the line joining the charges
- Opposite charges attract, same charges repel
- Forces are vectors → direction matters

---

## 🧮 Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Understand the setup
We have:
- Four charges: +1 C at the corners of a square
- One charge: -2 C at the center
- Side length: a = 1 m

We are asked: **What is the net force on the center charge?**

---

### Step 2: Find distance from center to a corner

From geometry of a square:

r = a / √2 = 1 / √2

This is the distance between the center charge and each corner charge.

---

### Step 3: Calculate force from ONE charge

Using Coulomb’s Law:

F = k |q₁q₂| / r²

Substitute:
q₁ = +1 C  
q₂ = -2 C  

F = k(2) / (1/2) = 4k

So each corner exerts a force of magnitude **4k** on the center.

---

### Step 4: Determine direction of forces

- The center charge is **negative**
- Corner charges are **positive**

👉 So all forces are **attractive**, pointing **toward each corner**

---

### Step 5: Use symmetry

This is the key step:

- Forces from opposite corners are equal in magnitude
- Their directions are opposite

So they cancel **pairwise**

Example:
- Top-left cancels bottom-right
- Top-right cancels bottom-left

---

### Step 6: Add all forces

Total force = 0

---

## ✅ Final Answer
F_net = 0

---

## 💡 Key Insight
Even though each individual force is strong, **perfect symmetry causes complete cancellation**.
