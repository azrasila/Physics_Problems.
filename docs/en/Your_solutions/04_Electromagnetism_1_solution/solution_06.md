# Problem 6 — Electric Field from Two Charges

## 📘 Theory
Electric field from a point charge:

E = kq / r²  

Total field:

E_total = E₁ + E₂ (vector sum)

---

## 🧮 Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Understand superposition
Each charge creates its own field.

Total field = vector sum of all contributions.

---

### Step 2: Write expressions

E₁ = kq₁ / r₁²  
E₂ = kq₂ / r₂²  

---

### Step 3: Direction matters
- Fields point away from + charges  
- Toward - charges  

---

### Step 4: Condition for zero field

For cancellation:

E₁ = E₂  

kq₁ / r₁² = kq₂ / r₂²  

---

### Step 5: Simplify

q₁ / r₁² = q₂ / r₂²  

---

### Step 6: Limit case

As distance increases:

r → ∞ ⇒ E → 0  

---

## ✅ Final Answer
Field cancels when magnitudes are equal and directions opposite.

---

## 💡 Key Insight
Electric field is a **vector → direction is crucial**
