# Problem 2 — Electric Potential

## 📘 Theory
Electric potential is defined as:

V = k Σ(qᵢ / r)

- It is a **scalar quantity**
- No direction → just algebraic sum
- Easier than force problems

---

## 🧮 Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Understand the setup
Charges at corners:
+1 C, -2 C, +3 C, -4 C

We need the **potential at the center**

---

### Step 2: Distance to center

All charges are equally distant:

r = 1 / √2

---

### Step 3: Write full expression

V = k (q₁/r + q₂/r + q₃/r + q₄/r)

Factor out k/r:

V = (k / r)(q₁ + q₂ + q₃ + q₄)

---

### Step 4: Sum the charges

1 - 2 + 3 - 4 = -2

---

### Step 5: Substitute

V = (k / (1/√2)) × (-2)

---

### Step 6: Simplify

V = -2k√2

---

## ✅ Final Answer
V = -2√2 k

---

## 💡 Key Insight
Because potential is scalar:
👉 **No vector cancellation needed — just add charges**
